### 0001. Useful Not True
  - "Useful Not True" is about reframing.
  - "有用但不真实"是关于重构。

  ---
  - Success in anything starts with your perspective which affects your strategy — your actions.
  - 任何事情的成功都始于你的观点，它会影响你的策略——你的行动。

  ---
  - Your first thought ("this is a disaster") feels true, but it’s not the only perspective.
  - 你的第一个想法（"这是一场灾难"）感觉很真实，但这并不是唯一的观点。

  ---
  - Your first thought is an obstacle you need to get past by realizing no thoughts are necessarily true.
  - 你的第一个想法是一个障碍，你需要通过认识到没有任何想法一定是正确的来克服它。

  ---
  - After your initial impulse, consider other perspectives, then choose the thought that’s more useful to you — the one that makes you take effective actions.
  - 在你最初的冲动之后，考虑其他观点，然后选择对你更有用的想法——让你采取有效行动的想法。

  ---
  - Understanding others: People share perspectives, not facts.
  - 理解他人： 人们分享观点，而不是事实。

  ---
  - They tell you how they see things.
  - 他们告诉你他们如何看待事物。

  ---
  - Like someone across the world telling you the time.
  - 就像世界各地的某个人告诉你时间一样。

  ---
  - Maybe it’s true for them, but not for you, and not for most other people.
  - 也许这对他们来说是正确的，但对你来说不是，对大多数其他人来说也不是。

  ---
  - Brains lie to their owners.
  - 大脑对它们的主人撒谎。

  ---
  - Nobody knows the real reasons why they do anything.
  - 没有人知道他们做任何事情的真正原因。

  ---
  - When someone says, "I believe…", then whatever they say next is not a fact.
  - 当有人说"我相信……"时，无论他们接下来说什么都不是事实。

  ---
  - No beliefs are necessarily true.
  - 没有任何信念一定是正确的。

  ---
  - Beliefs are perspectives.
  - 信念是观点。

  ---
  - Explanations are confabulated.
  - 解释都是虚构的。

  ---
  - Obligations are wishes.
  - 义务就是愿望。

  ---
  - Rules are arbitrary.
  - 规则是任意的。

  ---
  - They’re useful, but not necessarily true.
  - 它们很有用，但不一定是真的。

  ---
  - Knowing this gives you empathy, as you understand people’s incentives behind their beliefs.
  - 了解这一点会让你产生同理心，因为你了解人们信仰背后的动机。

### 0002. The Art of Spending Money: Simple Choices for a Richer Life
  - From the bestselling author of The Psychology of Money and Same as Ever, lessons on harnessing the power of money to live a happier life Most of us don’t know how to spend money.
  - 畅销书《金钱心理学》和《一如既往》的作者讲述了如何利用金钱的力量过上更幸福的生活。我们大多数人都不知道如何花钱。

  ---
  - We chase things that impress others but leave us cold.
  - 我们追逐那些给别人留下深刻印象的东西，但却让我们感到冷漠。

  ---
  - Or we save endlessly, afraid to spend on what would actually make life better.
  - 或者我们无休止地存钱，害怕花钱买真正能让生活变得更好的东西。

  ---
  - We confuse admiration with envy, comfort with excess, and utility with status.
  - 我们混淆了钦佩与嫉妒、舒适与过度、功利与地位。

  ---
  - The Art of Spending Money doesn't provide budgets, hacks, or one-size-fits-all solutions.
  - 《花钱的艺术》不提供预算、技巧或一刀切的解决方案。

  ---
  - It gives you understanding of how your relationship with money shapes your decisions—and how to reshape it so money works for you.
  - 它让你了解你与金钱的关系如何影响你的决定，以及如何重塑它，让金钱为你服务。

  ---
  - Morgan Housel’s work has helped millions rethink how they earn, save, and invest.
  - 摩根豪塞尔的工作帮助数百万人重新思考他们如何赚钱、储蓄和投资。

  ---
  - Now he turns his attention to the other side of the equation: how to spend.
  - 现在他将注意力转向等式的另一边：如何花钱。

  ---
  - With insight and warmth, he shows why the most valuable return on investment is peace of mind, why expectations matter more than income, and why doing well with money has less to do with spreadsheets and more to do with self-awareness.
  - 他以洞察力和热情向人们展示了为什么最有价值的投资回报是内心的平静，为什么期望比收入更重要，以及为什么用好钱与电子表格关系不大，而更多地与自我意识有关。

  ---
  - This book isn’t about getting rich.
  - 这本书不是关于致富的。

  ---
  - It’s about getting the most out of what you already have—and learning to want what’s worth wanting.
  - 这是关于充分利用你已经拥有的东西，并学会想要那些值得想要的东西。
